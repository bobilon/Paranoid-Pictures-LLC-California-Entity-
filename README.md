Paranoid Pictures LLC (California) — Entity Entry
The US node, linked to the UK Paranoid Pictures through Paul Gay
An addition to the Banksy corporate set: the first documented United States corporate node, established through a name-and-officer correspondence with the UK Paranoid Pictures entity. Documented facts and tagged interpretation kept separate throughout.

What the record shows
Paranoid Pictures LLC — California Secretary of State entity 200108710093. Articles of Organization signed 25 January 2001, filed 5 March 2001. Stated business purpose: "Directorial services." Registered agent and member: Paul Gay, an individual residing in California (1658 10th St, Santa Monica). Member-managed. Cancelled by Certificate of Cancellation filed December 2002, signed 11 October 2002 by "Paul Roger Gay – agent," wound up through a Los Angeles accountant (Bruce Weber & Assoc., 6300 Wilshire Blvd). Lifespan: roughly January 2001 to late 2002.
This is the first documented US-registered entity in the Banksy corporate set, and the cancellation certificate settles its link to the UK side beyond inference: it is signed Paul Roger Gay — the full name matching exactly the documented principal of Paranoid Pictures Limited (UK, company 03641529), the British motion-picture-production vehicle. Not "a Paul Gay" who might be the same man; the same full name on both filings. The "Paranoid Pictures" identity was Gay's working production brand on both sides of the Atlantic — a UK company and a California "directorial services" LLC, both his.
The name itself later became publicly famous as the fictional production-company banner of Exit Through the Gift Shop (2010) — the Paramount-parody logo, bullet holes for stars, that opens the film. So "Paranoid Pictures" operated at three levels: Gay's UK company, Gay's California LLC, and the on-screen production identity Banksy adopted for the film. The crucial point this documents: the name was not invented for Exit. It was Paul Gay's pre-existing production brand, which the film adopted.
What the dates do and do not support
The lifespan is now documented and precise, and it is short: formed January 2001, filed March 2001, cancelled by late 2002. That single fact ends the most tempting reading cleanly.
It is tempting to read the 2001 formation as the moment Exit Through the Gift Shop went into development — an early pre-production vehicle, with Gay carrying the project through its development phase. That reading is impossible, and the cancellation date proves it: the LLC was cancelled in late 2002, while Exit documents events — Thierry Guetta's filming of street artists, his meeting with Banksy, his transformation into "Mr. Brainwash" — that ran roughly 2006 to 2008. The company was dead for four years before the film's subject matter even began. It cannot have been an Exit development vehicle, because it no longer existed when there was anything of Exit to develop.
What the record actually shows is simpler and better documented: Paul Gay, a commercial director, ran a California "directorial services" LLC under his established Paranoid Pictures brand for about two years (2001–2002), paralleling his UK company of the same name, then wound it up. When Exit was made later in the decade, it adopted the Paranoid Pictures name — a name that already belonged to a working director in the set. The name was reused for the film; the LLC had been closed for years.
The proximity of the 5 March 2001 filing to the 2001 Peace Is Tough show (roughly two weeks later) is noted, but date proximity is not a link: a Gay directorial-services LLC registering in March 2001 and a Glasgow art show two weeks later share a calendar window and nothing else on the record. Likewise, the source-relayed claim that an Exit script "was in the works by the millennium" is contradicted twice over — by the film's subject matter (a film about Guetta could not be scripted before Guetta's story occurred) and now by this LLC's cancellation in 2002. If there was an earlier, separate street-art-film concept that later evolved into Exit, that is a distinct and undocumented claim, and this LLC — a directorial-services vehicle dead by 2002 — does not establish it.
Where it sits in the set
Documented: a California "directorial services" LLC, Paranoid Pictures LLC, formed January 2001, filed 5 March 2001, cancelled late 2002, agent and member Paul Gay — confirmed by the cancellation certificate's signature as Paul Roger Gay, the same full name as the principal of the UK Paranoid Pictures Limited. The Paranoid Pictures identity was Gay's working production brand on both sides of the Atlantic, pre-dating Exit, which later adopted the name. A genuine US corporate node and a documented transatlantic link through a confirmed shared individual.
Tagged / not supported: that the 2001 formation marks Exit's development (impossible — the LLC was cancelled in 2002, four years before the film's subject matter began); that the Peace Is Tough date proximity is meaningful; and that an Exit script existed at the millennium (contradicted by both the timeline and the LLC's 2002 cancellation). These are flagged as interpretation the record contradicts.

Method note: the California LLC, its filing date, its agent, and the link to the UK Paranoid Pictures through Paul Gay are documented at the California Secretary of State and Companies House. The dating of Exit's development to 2001 is not supported, because the film's documented subject matter postdates the LLC by five years. The name was reused; the entity was not an early development vehicle. The discipline is the deliverable.
